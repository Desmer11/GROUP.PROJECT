How Does Blockchain Work?

Blockchain is a decentralized digital ledger that records transactions across many computers in a way that ensures the security and transparency of the data. Each transaction is recorded in a "block" and added to a "chain" of previous transactions, hence the name blockchain. This technology relies on cryptographic principles to link and secure the blocks, making it nearly impossible to alter or delete existing records without the consensus of the entire network.

Each block contains:

  • Transaction data: The details of the transactions, such as sender, receiver, and amount.
  • Timestamp: The time at which the transaction was conducted.
  • Hash: A unique identifier for the block generated by a cryptographic algorithm.
  • Previous block's hash: This links the block to the previous one, creating a chain of blocks.

Blockchain is often used for cryptocurrencies, but it has many other applications such as supply chain management, voting systems, and digital identity verification.

What is a Cryptocurrency?

Cryptocurrency is a type of digital or virtual currency that uses cryptography for security. Unlike traditional currencies issued by governments (fiat currencies), cryptocurrencies operate on decentralized networks based on blockchain technology. The most well-known cryptocurrency is Bitcoin, but there are thousands of others, including Ethereum, Litecoin, and Ripple.

Key characteristics of cryptocurrencies include:

  • Decentralization: They are typically not controlled by any central authority, such as a government or financial institution.
  • Security: Cryptographic techniques secure transactions and control the creation of new units.
  • Transparency: All transactions are recorded on a public ledger, which anyone can view and verify.
  • Anonymity: Users can make transactions without revealing their identities.

Cryptocurrencies are used for various purposes, including as a medium of exchange, a store of value, and an investment asset. They have also given rise to new financial innovations like decentralized finance (DeFi) and non-fungible tokens (NFTs).
